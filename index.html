Q&A

Three main different between Web1, Web2, Web3:

1. Interaction Type -

Each web represents a different generation of the internet, with each characterized by different levels of interaction with the user. In fact, upon close examination, it appears that over the years, interaction and technological capabilities have improved and changed.
Web1 was primarily static, meaning that those who created the content were the website owners themselves, resulting in a less active user experience.
Then, Web2 became slightly more attractive to users since the content was user-generated, despite companies and corporations controlling data management.
Finally, Web3, built using blockchain technology, primarily operates on a distributed data model, leading to a high level of user privacy. In essence, the development over the years has allowed users to engage in more complex interactions, enabling them to maintain their data and use distributed applications.


2. Data Control -

It seems that with the development of the web over the years, there has been a difference in data control between the versions. Over time, there has been less reliance on central servers/government authorities, and control over data has primarily shifted to users.
In Web1, data was controlled by central servers, and control was in the hands of the website owners rather than the users.
In Web2, despite the transition from static to a more attractive user experience, large technology companies still controlled user data and profited from it.
In Web3, however, since usage is based on blockchain technology, data storage is decentralized, giving users control and ownership of their data, enhancing their privacy and independence from central authorities.


3. Technological Usage -

Over the years, we can also see changes and advancements in each new version of the web.
In Web1, usage was mainly through basic technologies like HTML and CSS, primarily aimed at providing content to the site, resulting in static sites.
In Web2, there was a shift to slightly more innovative technologies like JavaScript and AJAX, leading to more dynamic usage due to the applications they supported.
In Web3, there was a transition to more advanced technology, namely blockchain, smart contracts, and tokens. This transition led to higher privacy due to increased security levels on the internet, user autonomy, and the promotion of applications on distributed networks, where control is not exerted by any party but the user themselves.


Use case example of "looking for adverse drug reactions", differents between Webs:

Web1

Static: Imagine visiting a website in Web1 dedicated to adverse drug reactions. The content would likely be static, presenting general information about common reactions without real-time updates or user contributions.

Limited Interaction: Users would have limited interaction, perhaps only being able to navigate through a series of static pages with information presented in a one-way manner.

Web2

Dynamic: In Web2, a website dedicated to adverse drug reactions would be more dynamic. Users might encounter features like forums or comment sections where they can share personal experiences and discuss reactions with others.

Enhanced Interaction: Users could actively engage by asking questions, sharing their stories, and interacting with other users. This dynamic interaction could provide valuable insights and support for individuals seeking information about adverse reactions.

Web3

Decentralization: In Web3, the landscape for adverse drug reactions could be decentralized. Imagine a platform built on blockchain technology where users have control over their data and interactions.

Advanced Interaction: Users could access decentralized applications (dApps) specifically designed to provide real-time updates on adverse reactions, smart contracts ensuring trustless transactions of information, and AI-powered systems for personalized recommendations and insights.
